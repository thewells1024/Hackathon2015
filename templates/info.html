Here are some tips to get you started:

  • Keep it to one page

    • This lets employers parse the form quickly and prevents attention loss.

    • Also, no one wants to have to flip throuh stapled pages. One page is clean and concise.

  • Put the most important things first

    • Make sure your contact information is easily noticable at the top of the page.

    • Highlight your education, work experience, skills, and relevant projects above most things.

    • Exceptions to this rule include very prominent and reknown awards you've received.

  • Use action words

    • Start line items with words such as 'organized,' 'led,' and 'developed.'

    • Avoid too much repetition of your action words, make sure to mix it up.

    • Also avoid uninspired words such as 'used' and 'did.'
